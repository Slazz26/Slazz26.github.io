Hackathon Projects

Finalist at Rennes Data Challenge 2025

Our team was a finalist in the 'Best Project' category at the Rennes Data Challenge by developing a solution for Enedis in just 24 hours. Faced with the strategic challenge of resource planning, the problem was to determine if Enedis would have sufficient operational capacity and to identify potential bottlenecks. We designed a predictive model using seasonal Prophet models to anticipate work hour needs by activity, month, and skill for the year 2025.

Artificial Intelligence Projects

Classification of Rap by Linguistic Analysis

I developed an NLP model capable of distinguishing rap from other musical genres by analyzing lyrics with 90% accuracy. This system uses TF-IDF vectorization and SVM/RandomForest classifiers to automatically identify distinctive linguistic characteristics of rap. Text preprocessing and lemmatization allow for detailed analysis of lexical density, rhetorical patterns, and vocabulary specific to this musical genre.

Flappy Bird Mastered by Evolving Neural Networks

This project explores evolutionary artificial intelligence applied to the Flappy Bird game using the NEAT algorithm. I implemented an environment where neural networks evolve through natural selection, learning to navigate by analyzing the space between obstacles and the bird's position. The results are remarkable: after just a few generations, the best networks can play indefinitely.
